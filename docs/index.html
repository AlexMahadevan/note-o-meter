Elizabeth Warren · Jun 12, 2026

““If you live near one of these large data centers, your electricity bills over the last five years have gone up by as much as 267%."”

No, that’s not true: Electricity costs have risen in areas with high data center activity, but not by as much as Sen. Elizabeth Warren said.

Proposed Community Note 203/280
The 267% figure refers to wholesale electricity costs, not residential bills. Data center demand has raised consumer bills in high-activity areas, but not by the 267% cited for household bills. Source: https://www.politifact.com/factchecks/2026/jun/12/elizabeth-warren/data-centers-rising-electricity-costs/

Donald Trump · Jun 8, 2026

“The ongoing ballot counting in California means “they’re cheating on the election."”

No, that’s not true: As ballot counting continues in the LA mayor’s race and California governor’s race, we fact-checked President Donald Trump’s falsehood that the pace of the count is proof of cheating.

Proposed Community Note 259/280
Slow ballot counting in California is not evidence of cheating. The state counts ballots according to state law, a process that takes longer than most states because most California voters cast mail ballots, including many submitted on Election Day. Source: https://www.politifact.com/factchecks/2026/jun/08/donald-trump/California-election-LA-governor-Spencer-Pratt/

“Los Angeles mayoral candidate Spencer Pratt “got 0 out of 24,000 votes in a late night (Los Angeles) ballot drop.””

No, that’s not true: A lag in automated data from a media outlet caused the mix-up that briefly and mistakenly showed no new votes for Spencer Pratt in the Los Angeles mayoral race.

Proposed Community Note 265/280
This stems from a temporary data lag in the Associated Press's automated reporting, which briefly updated other candidates' numbers before Pratt's in the LA mayoral primary.
