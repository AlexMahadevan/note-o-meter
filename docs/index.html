Note-O-Meter Built on PolitiFact fact-checks

PolitiFact fact-checks, ready to file as Community Notes

Community Notes are reader-written fact-checks that attach to misleading posts on X, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube. Note-O-Meter drafts one for every false claim PolitiFact debunks — copy it, find the posts spreading the claim, and file your note.

  1. 1

    PolitiFact debunks it

    A claim spreading online gets rated False or Pants on Fire.

  2. 2

    We draft the note

    Claude turns the fact-check into a short, neutral note that fits the 280-character limit.

  3. 3

    You file it

    Copy the draft, find posts making the claim, edit, and add your Community Note.

Draft notes are written by AI (Claude) from PolitiFact's reporting. Treat them as a starting point: read the fact-check and edit before you file.

Elizabeth Warren · Jun 12, 2026

““If you live near one of these large data centers, your electricity bills over the last five years have gone up by as much as 267%."”

No, that’s not true: Electricity costs have risen in areas with high data center activity, but not by as much as Sen. Elizabeth Warren said.

Proposed Community Note 203/280
The 267% figure refers to wholesale electricity costs, not residential bills. Data center demand has raised consumer bills in high-activity areas, but not by the 267% cited for household bills. Source: https://www.politifact.com/factchecks/2026/jun/12/elizabeth-warren/data-centers-rising-electricity-costs/

Donald Trump · Jun 8, 2026

“The ongoing ballot counting in California means “they’re cheating on the election."”

No, that’s not true: As ballot counting continues in the LA mayor’s race and California governor’s race, we fact-checked President Donald Trump’s falsehood that the pace of the count is proof of cheating.

Proposed Community Note 259/280
Slow ballot counting in California is not evidence of cheating. The state counts ballots according to state law, a process that takes longer than most states because most California voters cast mail ballots, including many submitted on Election Day. False
X posts · Jun 8, 2026

“Los Angeles mayoral candidate Spencer Pratt “got 0 out of 24,000 votes in a late night (Los Angeles) ballot drop.””

No, that’s not true: A lag in automated data from a media outlet caused the mix-up that briefly and mistakenly showed no new votes for Spencer Pratt in the Los Angeles mayoral race.

Proposed Community Note 265/280
This stems from a temporary data lag in the Associated Press's automated reporting, which briefly updated other candidates' numbers before Pratt's in the LA mayoral primary. The complete data showed Pratt received 21,870 votes in an election night update. Source: https://www.politifact.com/factchecks/2026/jun/08/tweets/spencer-pratt-ballot-dump-zero-votes-24000/
